# Greenhouse style

How to write as Greenhouse. Where the Content Design guide and the marketing/editorial guide collide, Content Design wins.

## Voice

Four traits, always on:

- **Insightful** — surface the right information at the right time; write like a subject-matter expert.
- **Human** — write like you're helping a friend: contractions, plain language, empathy.
- **Candid** — direct, no hedging or embellishment, even when the message is hard. Drop "maybe", "possibly", "might want to consider".
- **Optimistic** — look forward, focus on what's possible; never forced.

## Tone

Tone is situational — match it to the reader and the moment. Two levers: direct ↔ enthusiastic, concise ↔ detailed. Be more direct and careful for sensitive moments (money, destructive actions, bad news). Calibrate with: bold not aggressive, confident not boastful, expert not arrogant, conversational not casual, curious not silly.

## Mechanics

- **Active voice.** Default to it; passive only for sensitive info, an unknown subject, or to avoid blaming the reader.
- **Sentence case** for headings, subheadings, and body; title case only for proper nouns and product names. Headings take no end punctuation unless they're a question.
- **Contractions** — use them.
- **Second person** — address the reader as "you"; use "they/them" for an unspecified third person.
- **Dashes** — spaced em dash ( — ) for a break or aside; en dash for ranges.
- **No Oxford comma** — "tools, expertise and resources." Use one only to prevent genuine ambiguity.
- **Numbers** — no ordinal dates ("April 6", not "6th"); commas for 1,000 and up; "%" for percentages.
- **Lists** — start each bullet with a capital, keep them parallel, punctuate consistently (all end in a period or none).
- **American English** — optimize, favorite, gray.

## Product and naming

- **Greenhouse** is always capitalized — it's both the company and the product. Don't make it possessive ("the Greenhouse hiring platform", not "Greenhouse's").
- Use full product names — **Greenhouse Recruiting**, **Greenhouse Onboarding** — never split off ("Recruiting") or call a product "the platform".
- Lowercase industry terms: talent acquisition, structured hiring, enterprise.

## Inclusive language

- People are "right" for a role, never "best"; teams gain a "culture add", never a "culture fit".
- Gender-neutral by default: "you", or "they/them".
- Person-first for disability ("person with a disability"); skip harsh idioms ("killing it").
- Lead positive — avoid negative framing and absolute claims.

## When in doubt

Merriam-Webster for spelling; the AP Stylebook for any punctuation or grammar not covered here.
